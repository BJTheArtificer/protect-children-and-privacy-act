CHAMBER RULES COMPANION TO THE
ARKANSAS PROTECTS OUR CHILDREN AND OUR PRIVACY ONLINE ACT

Companion to Senate Bill ___ / House Bill ___ (Arkansas Protects Our Children
and Our Privacy Online Act), implementing the companion rules mechanism
requested in § 4-88-1314(g)(7) of the Arkansas Code as proposed by that act.

This document contains two parallel resolutions:
  Part A — House Resolution amending the Rules of the House of Representatives
  Part B — Senate Resolution amending the Rules of the Senate

The two resolutions are substantively identical, varying only in the names of
the chamber, the presiding officer, and the chamber-specific rule references.
Both should be adopted in coordination with passage of the underlying act so
that the procedural protections of § 4-88-1314(g) take effect in each chamber
no later than the operative compliance date of the act.

PART A — HOUSE RESOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

State of Arkansas
95th General Assembly
Regular Session, 2025

HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. ___

By: Representative ________________________________

A RESOLUTION TO AMEND THE RULES OF THE ARKANSAS HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES TO
ESTABLISH PROCEDURAL PROTECTIONS FOR THE SCOPE LIMITATION ESTABLISHED BY
§ 4-88-1314 OF THE ARKANSAS CODE; TO ESTABLISH A POINT OF ORDER AGAINST A
BILL THAT WOULD AMEND, REPEAL, WEAKEN, OR EXTEND THAT SCOPE LIMITATION
WITHOUT COMPLYING WITH THE PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENTS OF § 4-88-1314(g); AND
FOR OTHER PURPOSES.

Subtitle

TO PROTECT THE PRIVACY ARCHITECTURE
OF THE ARKANSAS PROTECTS OUR CHILDREN
AND OUR PRIVACY ONLINE ACT THROUGH
HOUSE RULES PROCEDURAL PROTECTIONS.

WHEREAS, the General Assembly has enacted the Arkansas Protects Our Children
and Our Privacy Online Act, codified in part at § 4-88-1314 of the Arkansas
Code, which strictly limits the program established under that act to the
determination of whether a user is an adult and prohibits administrative
extension of the program to minor-side identification, profiling, restriction,
or behavioral control without affirmative authorization by act of the General
Assembly; and

WHEREAS, § 4-88-1314(g) establishes procedural expectations for the orderly
consideration of any future bill that would modify the scope limitation
established by that section, including a single-subject requirement, a
separate committee hearing requirement, a recorded vote requirement, a public
statement of effect requirement, and a fiscal and privacy impact statement
requirement; and

WHEREAS, § 4-88-1314(g)(7) requests that the House of Representatives and the
Senate each adopt, as part of the rules of the chamber, procedural rules
implementing those requirements, including without limitation a point of
order that may be raised on the floor of the chamber against a bill that
does not comply with those requirements; and

WHEREAS, the House of Representatives finds that the privacy and child-
welfare goals of the Arkansas Protects Our Children and Our Privacy Online
Act are best protected by parallel procedural rules in the House,
independently enforceable by point of order, that raise the deliberative cost
of any future modification of the scope limitation established by
§ 4-88-1314;

NOW THEREFORE,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:

SECTION 1. The Rules of the House of Representatives are amended by adding
a new rule to read as follows:

Rule ___. Procedural Protection of the Scope Limitation under
§ 4-88-1314 of the Arkansas Code.

(a) Definitions. — As used in this rule:
(1) "Covered bill" means a bill, resolution, or other measure that would,
if enacted:
(A) Amend, repeal, weaken, or otherwise modify the scope limitation
established by § 4-88-1314 of the Arkansas Code;
(B) Extend the program established under § 4-88-1306 et seq. of the
Arkansas Code to minor-side identification, profiling, restriction, or
behavioral control;
(C) Authorize the Office of State Technology, an approved issuer, or any
other person to use the state-certified interface or any credential
issued under § 4-88-1306 et seq. of the Arkansas Code for any of the
purposes prohibited by § 4-88-1314(b)(2); or
(D) Otherwise modify the procedural requirements established by
§ 4-88-1314(g).

(2) "Scope-limitation impact statement" means a written statement,
prepared by the Office of State Technology in consultation with the
Attorney General as required by § 4-88-1314(g)(6), addressing the matters
identified in that subdivision.

(b) Filing requirements. — A covered bill shall be filed as a standalone
bill addressing only the subject matter described in subsection (a) of
this rule, and shall not be combined with provisions addressing additional
subjects. A covered bill shall not be included as a provision within an
omnibus bill, an appropriations bill, a budget reconciliation measure, a
revenue bill, or any other bill addressing additional subjects.

(c) Statement on the face of the bill. — A covered bill shall include, on
the first page of the bill and prominently displayed, a plainly worded
statement identifying the bill as one that modifies or extends the scope
limitation established by § 4-88-1314 of the Arkansas Code, summarizing
the effect of the modification or extension, and citing § 4-88-1314(g)(5)
of the Arkansas Code.

(d) Hearing and notice. — A covered bill shall not be reported out of any
committee until:
(1) The committee has held a separate hearing on the bill, dedicated to
the bill alone or to other covered bills addressing the same subject;
(2) Public notice of the hearing has been published not less than fourteen
(14) days before the hearing, identifying the bill, summarizing its
effect, and providing the time and place of the hearing; and
(3) The scope-limitation impact statement required by § 4-88-1314(g)(6)
of the Arkansas Code has been received by the committee and made publicly
available not less than seven (7) days before the hearing.

(e) Recorded vote on final passage. — A vote on final passage of a covered
bill shall be a recorded roll-call vote.

(f) Point of order. — At any time before final passage of a covered bill,
any member of the House may raise a point of order that the bill, or the
process by which it has been considered, fails to comply with this rule
or with the requirements of § 4-88-1314(g) of the Arkansas Code. The
Speaker shall rule on the point of order. If the Speaker finds that the
bill or the process fails to comply with this rule or with
§ 4-88-1314(g):
(1) The bill shall not be advanced further until the noncompliance has
been cured;
(2) If the noncompliance is the inclusion of covered subject matter in a
bill addressing additional subjects, the chair of the committee of
jurisdiction may sever the covered subject matter into a standalone
companion bill subject to this rule, and the original bill may proceed
without the severed provisions; and
(3) An appeal of the ruling of the Speaker shall require an affirmative
vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the members of the House present and voting
to overturn.

(g) Suspension of this rule. — This rule shall not be suspended except by
an affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the members elected to the
House, taken by recorded roll-call vote, with notice on the calendar of
not less than three (3) legislative days before the vote on suspension.

(h) Findings. — The House of Representatives finds that:
(1) The procedural requirements established by this rule are appropriate
to ensure that any future modification of the scope limitation established
by § 4-88-1314 of the Arkansas Code receives full deliberative
consideration consistent with the importance of the subject matter to the
privacy of lawful adult Arkansans, the welfare of Arkansas minors and
their families, and the integrity of the Arkansas Protects Our Children
and Our Privacy Online Act;
(2) The two-thirds-vote requirement for suspension of this rule and for
overturning a ruling of the Speaker on a point of order under this rule
is intended to prevent erosion of the procedural protections established
by this rule through ordinary legislative action; and
(3) Nothing in this rule purports to limit the constitutional authority
of a future House of Representatives to amend its own rules by the
procedures established by the Arkansas Constitution and the Rules of the
House.

SECTION 2. This Resolution is effective on adoption.

DRAFTING NOTES (NOT PART OF EITHER RESOLUTION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Rule numbering. — The "Rule ___" placeholder in each Part should be filled
in with the next available rule number in the chamber's existing numbering
scheme at the time of adoption. Each chamber's parliamentarian or rules
committee should assign the number consistent with existing organization of
the chamber's rules.

2. Two-thirds threshold. — The two-thirds threshold for suspension of the
rule and for overturning a Speaker/President ruling is a stronger threshold
than the simple majority normally required to suspend a procedural rule. It
is the highest threshold typically used by legislative chambers for rules
suspension and is the principal mechanism by which this rule resists
erosion. A future chamber retains constitutional authority to amend the rule
by simple majority at the start of a session, but cannot circumvent it
mid-session by ordinary motion.

3. Severability of covered subject matter. — Subsection (f)(2) of each rule
permits severance of covered provisions from a non-compliant omnibus bill
into a standalone companion bill. This avoids using the point of order as a
nuclear option that kills entire unrelated bills, while still enforcing the
single-subject requirement of § 4-88-1314(g)(2). The committee chair has
discretion whether to sever or to abandon the covered provisions entirely.

4. Interaction with the underlying act. — The procedural requirements of
§ 4-88-1314(g) of the Arkansas Code apply by force of statute regardless of
whether these chamber rules are adopted. Adoption of the chamber rules adds
an independently enforceable mechanism (point of order) that operates within
each chamber's parliamentary process. A future legislature that fails to
follow § 4-88-1314(g) without these chamber rules in place may still face
challenges under the statute itself, including challenges based on the
fiscal and privacy impact statement requirement and the public statement of
effect requirement.

5. Constitutional limits acknowledged. — The findings in subsection (h)(3)
of each rule acknowledge that no chamber may bind a future chamber against
its constitutional authority to amend its own rules. This is included for
the same reason as the parallel acknowledgment in § 4-88-1314(g)(8) of the
underlying act: to ensure that the rule itself is not struck as an
unconstitutional entrenchment, which would leave nothing in place. The
practical protection comes from the political and procedural cost of
amending the rule, not from any claim of permanence.

6. Coordination with passage of the underlying act. — These resolutions
should be filed and adopted contemporaneously with passage of the
Arkansas Protects Our Children and Our Privacy Online Act, so that the
procedural protections of § 4-88-1314(g) are operative from the date of
enactment. Adoption may also occur at the start of any subsequent regular
session as part of the standard rules adoption process.

7. Cross-chamber coordination. — Both chambers should adopt substantively
identical resolutions to ensure that a covered bill faces consistent
procedural protections in either chamber of origin. The two parts of this
companion are drafted to be identical in substance and to vary only in
chamber-specific terminology.
