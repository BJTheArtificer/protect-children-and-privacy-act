# Contributing

Thank you for considering a contribution to this repository.

This is a working legislative draft. We genuinely want feedback, criticism, and improvement, and we recognize that the people most likely to spot problems with the bill — constitutional lawyers, security researchers, retail and POS technologists, civil liberties advocates, child safety researchers, fiscal analysts, drafters from other states — are largely not the people who wrote it.

## What we are looking for

**Substantive criticism.** Tell us what we got wrong. Specifically. With reasoning. We would rather find a problem now than after the bill is enacted.

**Drafting improvements.** Legislation lives or dies by its language. If you see ambiguous, contradictory, or sloppy drafting, point it out.

**Technical corrections.** The bill makes assumptions about how operating systems, app stores, retail POS systems, and credential lifecycle systems work. If those assumptions are wrong, we want to know.

**Constitutional analysis.** First Amendment, Section 230, dormant Commerce Clause, due process, and prior restraint are all live issues. Constitutional flaws should be flagged.

**Fiscal challenges.** The fiscal note is built from publicly available data and reasoned estimates. If you have access to better data, share it.

**Adaptations from other states.** If you are using this bill as a template for legislation in your jurisdiction and you ran into a problem we didn't anticipate, please tell us about it.

**Plain-English clarifications.** If a section is hard for a non-lawyer to understand, that is a defect we want to fix.

## How to contribute

**Open an issue** for problems, questions, or discussion. Be specific about what section, what concern, and (if applicable) what alternative you would propose.

**Open a pull request** for concrete drafting changes. Include a brief explanation of what you are changing and why.

**Fork the repository** if you want to adapt the bill for another jurisdiction. You don't need permission. The license allows it. We would appreciate a note (via issue or otherwise) so we can learn from your adaptation.

## What we will probably not accept

**Changes that weaken the boolean output requirement.** The single binary signal is the central privacy commitment of the bill. Proposals to allow age bands, exact ages, dates of birth, or persistent identifiers are unlikely to be accepted because they break the architecture.

**Changes that broaden compelled-intermediary remedies.** The bill is deliberately strict about court orders that direct ISPs or search engines to block content. Proposals to lower the threshold are unlikely to be accepted because we believe they create First Amendment and Section 230 exposure that the current language is structured to avoid.

**Changes that remove the sunset clause.** A regulatory program of this scope deserves regular legislative reexamination. The sunset is structural.

**Changes that remove the fee caps.** The caps in statute are how the program is kept from quietly growing into a revenue source.

If you disagree with any of these positions, by all means make the argument in an issue. Some of our positions may be wrong. But we want to be clear about which are negotiable and which are foundational.

## Code of conduct

Be civil. Disagree on substance, not on people. This is legislation that affects real children, real families, and real adults' privacy. Everyone working on it in good faith deserves respect.

## Maintainers

This repository is maintained by the bill's sponsors and a small group of contributors. Contact information will be added once the bill is formally filed.
